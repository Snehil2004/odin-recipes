Masala Dosa

Description

Masala dosa is a South Indian crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It's a crispy, thin dosa filled with a savory potato filling.

Ingredients:

    For the Dosa Batter:
  • Urad dal (whole black gram) - ½ cup
  • Parboiled rice - 2 cups
  • Fenugreek seeds - 1 teaspoon
  • Salt to taste
    • For the Potato Filling:
  • Potatoes - 2 medium, boiled and mashed
  • Onions - 1 medium, chopped
  • Green chilies - 1 (optional, for spice)
  • Curry leaves - few
  • Mustard seeds - 1 teaspoon
  • Urad dal (split black gram) - 1 teaspoon
  • Channa dal (split Bengal gram) - 1 teaspoon
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric powder - ½ teaspoon
  • Sambar powder - 1 tablespoon
  • Coriander powder - 1 teaspoon
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for cooking
Steps
    Making the Dosa Batter:
  1. Wash and soak urad dal and fenugreek seeds together for 6-8 hours.
  2. Wash and soak rice separately for 6-8 hours.
  3. Grind the soaked urad dal and fenugreek seeds into a fine batter.
  4. Grind the soaked rice into a separate batter.
  5. Combine both batters in a large bowl and mix well. Add salt and whisk until smooth.
  6. Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for 12-16 hours, or until doubled in size.
    7. Making the Potato Filling:
  7. Heat oil in a pan. Splutter mustard seeds, urad dal, and channa dal.
  8. Add curry leaves and chopped onions. Saute until onions turn translucent.
  9. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  10. Add turmeric powder, sambar powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  11. Add mashed potatoes and cook for a few minutes, mashing any lumps.
    12. Cooking the Masala Dosa:
  12. Heat a flat griddle or tawa. Grease it lightly with oil.
  13. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the griddle and spread it into a thin circle.
  14. Drizzle a little oil around the edges of the dosa.
  15. Once the dosa starts to cook and the bottom turns golden brown, spread some potato filling in the center.
  16. Fold the dosa into a half-moon shape.
  17. Cook for a minute more and serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
